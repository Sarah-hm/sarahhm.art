Artist Statement

As a tangible and digital practitioner, I develop creative tools and methodologies in various collaborative spaces. I explore the middle-ground between my passion for chaos (theory) and that of pattern making methodologies (e.g., garments, maps, taxonomy, creative coding) through Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). The audience (sometimes machine) takes control of the structures I build, allowing the output to reflect farther horizons than my own interpretation of the design. Collaboration can have chaotic tendencies; learning to lose control and create in that space is at the core of my practice.

I also learn to acknowledge my disposition and how it can best be used in amplifying voices. I focus my attention on folk ontologies; that is how people think things to be like as opposed to how they really are. I design interactive experiences that aim to simplify complex notions of our modern world and make knowledge more accessible. I find that candid interpretations of convoluted concepts allow more enchanting speculations to emerge. I aim for the tools I create to be structures not only operable by myself, but by other practitioners as well.

Bio

Sarah Hontoy-Major is a transdisciplinary digital and tangible practioner based in tiohtià:ke/Montreal. They ¸ interweave their pattern making knowledge and their coding practice in their art-based research on creative toolmaking. They have a Political Science degree (2017) from Dawson College, a Fashion design degree (2020) from Marie-Victorin College, and have been pursuing an undergraduate specialization in Computation Arts in Concordia University since 2020.

Sarah has worked as a 3D technician in the fashion industry as well as a Research Assistant in several wearable and design labs, namely Vestechpro, the UQAM Diament Research Chair in e-Mental Health, and for Fashion Designer Ying Gao. They have more recently joined the IFRC, AbTeC and Abundant Intelligences research programs exploring and developing Indigenous epistemologies and futurism in tech.

Their Femme (2022) piece was presented in the Design and Computation Arts Flux exhibition in 2022, and their Data Patterns (2022) project in the following year’s Strata exhibition. Their Shein’s Cabinet (2022) Google Extension was part of the _scapes mediation in Concordia’s 4th Space, where the weeklong Fine Arts in.site symposium they co-organized also took place. They became the president of the Computation Arts Student Society in 2024.

As Sarah combines their interest for unconventional digital-to-tangible creative tool-making and knowledge dissemination for non-expert audiences, they are currently working on Symphenia, an interactive emulation of the neuroscience of perceptions, set to be presented at the Glen Atrium of the McGill Health Research Institute in April 2024.
